ANTIMERIA, in Grammar, a figure whereby one part of speech is used for another: e. g. velle suam cuique est, for voluntas sua cuique est ; also populus late rex, for populus late regnans. Antimeria, in a more restrained sense, is a figure where the noun is repeated instead of the pronoun.
