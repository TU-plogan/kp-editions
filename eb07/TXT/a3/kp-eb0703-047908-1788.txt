ARENSWALDE, a circle in the government of Frankfort, and province of Pomerania, in Prussia. Its extent is 472 square miles, or 302,080 acres, and the population amounted in 1817 to 20,705 persons. It has a number of small lakes, which yield good fish. The corn land produces some rye, and the inhabitants are also employed in rearing and feeding cattle; but their means of subsistence is chiefly derived from the extensive woods with which it abounds.

Arenswalde, the chief town of the circle of the same name in Prussia. It contains 474 houses, and 2875 inhabitants, who find some employment in weaving cloth, and some in distilleries.

Arenz de Mar, a town of Spain, on the sea-shore, in the province of Catalonia. It contains 3500 inhabitants, and has manufactories for cotton, silk, woollen goods, and stockings. There is a yard for building ships, and a forge for casting anchors.
