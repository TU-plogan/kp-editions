ANNECY, a city in the kingdom of Sardinia, the capital of the province of Genevois, in the duchy of Savoy. It is at the foot of the mountain Semina, on the banks of the lake of that name. It is the most industrious place in Savoy, having manufactures of cotton goods, of hats, glass, and earthenware, and several distilleries and tanneries, and near it some iron-works. It contains a cathedral, a church, five monasteries, and the same number of nunneries.
