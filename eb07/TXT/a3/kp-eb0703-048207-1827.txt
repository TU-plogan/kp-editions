ARGUN, Argan, or Ergon, a river of Tartary, in the country of the Mongols, which rises from a lake called Dalai, or Koulon-nor, situated in long. 119. 14. E. and lat. 49. N. It is considered to be the original source of the Amur, and by the treaty of Nertschinsk, concluded in 1690, is established as the mutual boundary between the Chinese and Russian empires, from its source to its mouth.

It carries on a considerable trade, but is situated in an extremely cold and inhospitable country. In the neighbourhood are valuable silver mines, and fine gold is frequently extracted from the ore. 177 miles east of Nertschinsk.
