IVER, a town of the county of Buckingham, in the hundred of Stoke, eighteen miles from London. It stands on the river Colme, which turns several corn and some spinning mills. The population amounted in 1801 to 1377, in 1811 to 1635, in 1821 to 1663, and in 1831 to 1870.
