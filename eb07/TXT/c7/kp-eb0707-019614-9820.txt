CONCORD, in Grammar, that part of construction called syntax, in which the words of a sentence agree; that is, in which nouns are put in the same gender, number, and case, and verbs in the same number and person, with nouns, and pronouns. See Grammar. Concord, in Jfwsze, the relation of two sounds which are always agreeable to the ear, whether applied in succession or consonance.
