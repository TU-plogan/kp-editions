CONOVIUM, in Ancient Geography, a town of the Ordovices, in Britain. From its ruins arose, at the distance of four miles, Aberconway, at the mouth of the Conway, in Caernarvonshire; and on the spot where Conovium stood is an hamlet, called Caerhaen, or the old town. See Cambden.
