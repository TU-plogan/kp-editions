CULNA, a town of Bengal, on the northern bank of one of the numerous streams which constitute the delta of the Ganges. At this place boats generally stop to take in a stock of fresh water and provisions, and to procure a pilot to convey them through the intricate navigation of the Sunderbunds. Long. 89. 32. E.
