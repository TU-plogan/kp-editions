COOLOO, a mountainous and woody district of Hindustan, in the province of Lahore, bounded on the east by the Sutledge river, on the west by the Ravey, and nearly divided into two by the Beyah river, dt is situated between the 33d and 34th degrees of north latitude. The chief town is of the same name, and is situated on the eastern side of the Bengal river, 155 miles north-east from the city of Lahore. Long. 75.48. E. Lat. 33. 20. N. There are also two other towns of Hindustan of this name; one belonging to the independent Zemindars, in the province of Orissa, ninety miles west by north from Cuttuck, in long. 84. 40. E. lat. 20. 40. N.; and the other in the province of Cuttuck, situated on the south side of the Mahanuddy river, sixty miles west-south-west from Cuttuck. This is a large village, and is of considerable trade, importing cotton from Berar, and returning to the interior loaded with salt.
