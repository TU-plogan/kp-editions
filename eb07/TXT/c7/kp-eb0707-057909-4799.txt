CZORTKOW, a circle in the Austrian kingdom of Gallicia, in the eastern frontier, bordering on the Russian territory. Its extent is about 1450 square miles. It comprehends three cities, nineteen market-towns, and 242 villages. It contained, in 1817, 30,829 houses, and 149,488 inhabitants, of whom 7226 were Jews. Its rivers run by the Dniester into the Black Sea. It is a fertile district, abounding in wheat, maize, pulse, tobacco, aniseed, honey, wax, cattle, and all kinds of fruit.
