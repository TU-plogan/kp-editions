COMMISSION, in common law, the warrant or letters patent, which all persons exercising jurisdiction have to empower them to hear or determine any cause or suit; as the commission of the judges, and the like.

Commission of Bankrupt. See Bankruptcy. Commission of Lunacy issues out of the court of chancery, in order to try whether a person represented to be a lunatic be so or not. See Lunacy. Commission of Teinds, a court at Edinburgh, which came instead of a committee of the Scottish parliament, [7:7:177]for erecting new parishes, and valuing temds for the support of the clergy. It is vested in the lords of session. See Scotland. . Commission, in Commerce. See Factorage.
