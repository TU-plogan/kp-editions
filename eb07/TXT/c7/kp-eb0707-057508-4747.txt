CYPHONISM (Cyphonismus, from κupωv, which has various significations, derived from κυφος, crookedf a kind of torture or punishment in use among the ancients. The learned are at a loss to determine what it was. Some will have it to be that mentioned by Jerome in his Life of Paul the Hermit (chap. 2), which consisted in smearing the body with honey, and thus exposing the person so bedaubed, with his hands tied, to the warm sun, in order to invite the flies and other vermin to persecute him.
