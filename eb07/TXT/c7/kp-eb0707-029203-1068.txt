CONTE, a fortified city in the department of the North, in France, at the confluence of the two navigable rivers Hesne and Scheldt, by whose waters it may be easily surrounded, by means of sluices under the power of the city. It contains 600 houses, and 6080 inhabitants. The chief trade consists in building river craft. Long. 3. 30. E. Lat. 50. 56. N.
