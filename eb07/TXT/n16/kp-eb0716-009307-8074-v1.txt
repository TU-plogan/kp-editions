NEOTS, St, a market-town of the county of Huntingdon, in the hundred of Toesland, fifty-six miles from London. It stands on the navigable river Ouse, over which there is a fine stone bridge. It is a well-built place, with a large market-place, and has a handsome church, with an elegant and lofty spire. It has a good market, which is held on Thursday. The population amounted in 1801 to 1752, in 1811 to 1998, in 1821 to 2272,* and in 1831 to 2617.

Neots, St, a town of the county of Cornwall, in the hundred of West, distant 226 miles from London. It is said to have been built by a relation of King Allred, who died here in 899, and was buried at the place of the same name in Huntingdonshire. The population amounted in 1801 to 906, in 1811 to 1041, in 1821 to 1255, and in 1831 to 1424.
