ROAN in the manège. A roan horse is one of a bay, sorrel, or black colour, with gray or white spots interspersed very thick. When this party-cbloured coat is accompanied with a black head and black extremities, he is called α roan horse with a blackamoor’s head; and if the same mixture is predominant upon a deep sorrel, he is called claret-roan.
