FAVISSAE, in Antiquity, were, according to Festus and Aulus Gellius, cisterns for containing water. But the faviss(v in the capitol at Rome were subterranean cellars, where old statues, broken vessels, and other things used in the temple, were usually laid up. They were much the same with the archives and treasury of modern churches.
