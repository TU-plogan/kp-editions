FELUDJE, Felugia, a village of Asiatic Turkey, situated on the eastern shore of the Euphrates, north of Hil-Iah, in a fertile soil, productive in dates, grapes, grain, and cotton. Here a palace was erected by Soliman the Great, pasha of Bagdad. It was anciently a place of celebrity, and taken by the Romans under the Emperor Julian.
