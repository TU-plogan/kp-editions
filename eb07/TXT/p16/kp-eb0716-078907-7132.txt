PANIONIA, in Antiquity, a festival celebrated in honour of Neptune, by a concourse of people from all the cities of Ionia. In this festival, if the bull offered in sacrifice happened to bellow, it was accounted an omen of divine favour, because that sound was thought to be acceptable to Neptune.
