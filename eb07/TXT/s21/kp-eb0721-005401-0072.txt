SYMPLOCE, συμπλoxn, in Rhetoric, a figure, where the same word is repeated several times in the beginning and end of a sentence, including the Anaphora and Epirtophe. Thus, “Quis legem tulit? Rullus. Quis majorem populi partem suffragiis privavit? Rullus. Quis comitiis praefuit? Idem Rullus.”
