BAUGE, an arrondissement of the department of the Mayenne and the Loire, in France, extending over 668 square miles, and comprehending six cantons and sixty-one communes, with 75,793 inhabitants. The chief place, of the same name, is on the river Souanon, and contains 3350 inhabitants, employed principally in making serges and other woollen goods.
[7:4:456]

Bauge, a drugget manufactured in Burgundy with thick spun thread and coarse wool.
