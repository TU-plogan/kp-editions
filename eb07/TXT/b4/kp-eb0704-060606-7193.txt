BEVEL, among masons, carpenters, &c. a kind of square, one leg whereof is frequently crooked, according to the sweep of an arch or vault. It is movable on a centre, and so may be set to any angle.

lÌRVEr-Arigle, any angle excepting those of 45° or 90°.
