SORITES, in Logic, a species of reasoning, in which a great number of propositions are so linked together, that the predicate of the one becomes continually the subject of the next following, till at last a conclusion is formed by bringing together the subject of the first proposition and[7:20:482] the predicate of the last. Such was that merry argument of Themistoclee, to prove that his little son under ten years old governed the whole world: “My son governs his mother; his mother me; I the Athenians; the Athenians the Greeks; Greece commands Europe; Europe the whole world: therefore my son commands the whole world.”
