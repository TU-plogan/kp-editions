JUNAGUR, a town of Hindustan, in the province of Gujerat, possessed by an independent native chief, now one of the British allies. He and other chiefs engaged, in 1808, with the Bombay government, not to connive at piracy, and to permit a free and open commerce with the British vessels, on paying the stipulated duties.
