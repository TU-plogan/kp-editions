LIBNA, in Ancient Geography, a sacerdotal city in the f tribe of Judah, a place of strength, as appears from Sennacherib’s laying siege to it (2 Kings, xix.; Isaiah, xxxvii.). In Jerome’s time, it was a village, called Lobna, in the territory of Elcutheropolis∙
