LETHARGY, in Medicine (from oblivion, and agy∕α, numbness, laziness'), a disease consisting of a profound drowsiness or sleepiness, from which the patient can scarcely be awaked; or, if awaked, he remains stupid, without sense or memory, and presently sinks again into his former sleep.
