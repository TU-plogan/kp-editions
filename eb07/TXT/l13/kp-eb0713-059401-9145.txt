LUGDUNUM, in Ancient Geography, the capital of the Segusiani, in Gallia Celtica, situated on an eminence at the conflux of the Arar and Rhodanus, as the Celtic term dun indicates. It was built by Manutius Plancus, ujider Augustus, while commanding in that part of Gaul, and thither he led a colony. Lugdunum is now Lyons, capital of the Lyonnois.

Lugdunum Batavorum, in Ancient Geography, a town of the Batavi, in Gallia Belgica; now Leyden, in Holland.
