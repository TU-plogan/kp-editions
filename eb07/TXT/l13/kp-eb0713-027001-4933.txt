LIBERA, in Mythology, the name of a goddess, which Cicero, in his book De Naturd Deorum, represents as the daughter of Jupiter and Ceres. Ovid, in his Fasti, says, that the name was given by Bacchus to Ariadne. Libera is exhibited on medals as a kind of female Bacchus, crowned with vine leaves.
