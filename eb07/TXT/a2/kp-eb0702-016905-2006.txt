ADYTUM, in Pagan Antiquity, the most retired and sacred place of temples, into which none but the priests were allowed to enter. The Sanctum Sanctorum of the temple of Solomon was of the nature of the pagan adytum, none but the high-priest being admitted into it, and he but once a year.
