ACHOR, a valley of Jericho, lying along the river Jordan, not far from Gi≈Çgal; so called from Achan, the troubler of Israel, being there stoned to death.

Achor, in Medicine, a species of Herpes. Achor, in Mythology, the god of flies; to whom, according to Pliny, the inhabitants of Cyrene sacrificed, in order to obtain deliverance from the insects, and the disorders occasioned by them.
