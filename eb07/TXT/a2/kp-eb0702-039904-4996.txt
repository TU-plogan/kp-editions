ALENIO, Julius, a Jesuit, born at Brescia, in the republic of Venice. He travelled into the eastern countries, and arrived at Macao in 1610, where he taught mathematics. From thence he went to the empire of China, where he continued to propagate the Christian religion for 36 years. He was the first who planted the faith in die province of Xansi, and he built several churches in the province of Fokien. He died in August 1649, leaving behind him several works in the Chinese language.
