MAHMUDABAD, a town of Hindustan, in the province of Gujerat. This place was founded by Sultan Mahmoud of Gujerat in the fourteenth century. It contained many stately edifices, and was surrounded with a brick wall fourteen miles in extent; but of these there are now few remains. Long. 72. 52. E. Lat. 22. 47. N. It is also the name of a town in the province of Agra and district of Furruckabad. Long. 79. 25. E. Lat. 27. 19. N.
