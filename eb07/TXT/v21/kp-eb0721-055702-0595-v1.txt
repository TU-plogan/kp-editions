VANNES, an arrondissement in the department of Morbihan in France, extending over 638 square miles, comprehending eleven cantons, divided into seven communes, and containing a population of 125,898 in 1836. The capital is the city of the same name, which is connected with the gulf of Morbihan by a canal about two and a half miles in length. It is well fortified, is the seat of a bishop, has a cathedral and other three churches, 1850 houses, and 11,623 inhabitants in 1836.
