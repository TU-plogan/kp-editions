VALENTINIANS, a sect of Christian heretics, who sprung up in the second century, and were so called from their leader Valentinus. The Valentinians were only a branch of the Gnostics, who realised or personified the Platonic ideas concerning the Deity, whom they called Pleroma or Plenitude.
