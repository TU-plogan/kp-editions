HENERY Isle, a small island lying due south from Bombay. It is about 600 yards in circumference, and nearly of a circular form. It is fortified and well inhabited. In 1790 it belonged to Ragojee Angria, and was a principal rendezvous of private vessels, though within sight of Bombay. In 1790 it belonged to the Peshwa, and was also the haunt of pirates. Henery is situated in long. 72. 50. E. and lat. 18. 42. N.
