HELLENISTIC Language, that used by the Grecian Jews who lived in Egypt and other parts where the Greek tongue prevailed. In this language the Septuagint was written, and also the books of the New Testament; and it was thus denominated, to show that it was Greek filled with Hebraisms and Syriacisms.
