KOSLOW, a city of the Russian province Tambow, the capital of a circle of the same name, on the river Woronesh. It contains three wooden and five stone churches, a convent, and 1237 houses, with 8060 inhabitants, who trade in salted fish, hides, tallow, and other produce of the soil. Long. 40. 12. E. Lat. 54. 13. N.

Koslow, a city of the Russian province of Taurien, the capital of a circle of the same name. It stands at the bottom of a deep bay on the Black Sea. It has been declared a free port ever since 1798, and has a quarantine station.
