WALAJA, or Abadnagub, a town of the south of India, in the province of the Carnatic, situated on the north side of the Palar river. It is a place of considerable consequence, regularly built, rich and populous. By means of bullocks, it carries on a considerable trade between the seacoast and Mysore. Long. 78. 5. E. Lat. 12. 40.
