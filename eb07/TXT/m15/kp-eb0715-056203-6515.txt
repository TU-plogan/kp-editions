MORTIER, an ensign of dignity, which was borne by the chancellor and grand presidents of the parliament of France. That borne by the chancellor consisted of a piece of cloth of gold, edged and turned up with ermine; that borne by the first president was a piece of black velvet edged with a double row of gold lace.
