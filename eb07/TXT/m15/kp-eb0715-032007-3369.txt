MOLD, or Mould, a town of the county of Flint, in North Wales, in the hundred of its own name, 207 miles from London. It consists of one long and wide street, and has a market on Saturday. It is an ancient borough, and, in conjunction with Rhyddlan, Overton, Caerwis, Caergwr-ley, St Asaph, and Holywell, returns one member to the House of Commons. Of late extensive cotton-works have been established at this place. The population amounted in 1801 to 4235, in 1811 to 5083, in 1821 to 6268, and in 1831 to 8086.
