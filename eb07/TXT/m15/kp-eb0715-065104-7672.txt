MYCONUS, in Ancient Geography, one of the islands called Cyclades, near Delos, under which the last of the centaurs slain by Hercules are feigned to lie buried. Hence the proverb, Omnia sub unam Myconum congerere, applied to an injudicious or unnatural farrago.
