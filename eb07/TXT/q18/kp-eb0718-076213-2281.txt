QUADRIGA, in Antiquity, a ear or chariot drawn by four horses. On the reverses of medals we frequently see the Emperor or Victory in a quadriga, holding the reins of [7:18:763]the horses; and hence these coins are amongst the curious called nummi quadrigati and victoriati.
