QUINCUNX, in Roman antiquity, any thing that consists of five twelfths of another, but particularly of the as. Quincunx Order, in Gardeiing, is a plantation of trees, disposed originally in a square consisting of five trees, one at each corner, and a fifth in the middle; which disposition, repeated again and again, forms a regular grove, wood, or wilderness.
