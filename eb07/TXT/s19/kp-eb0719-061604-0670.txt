SAKKAR, a district of Hindustan, in the province of Bejapoor, belonging to the nizam, and situated about the seventeenth degree of north latitude. It is well watered, being situated between the rivers Beemah and Krishna, and is extremely fertile. To the north of the Beemah it is somewhat hilly. The principal towns are Nusseritabad and Sakkar; and the latter is the capital. It is situated on the north side of the Beemah river, sixty-five miles east from the city of Bejapoor. Long. 76. 38. E. Lat. 17. 4. N.
