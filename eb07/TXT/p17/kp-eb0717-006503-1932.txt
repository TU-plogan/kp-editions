PARALLEL, in Geometry, an appellation given to lines, surfaces, and bodies, which are everywhere equidistant from each other.

Parallel Sphere, that situation of the sphere in which the equator coincides with the horizon, and the poles with the zenith and nadir.

Parallel Sailing. See the article Navigation. Parallels of Latitude, in Astronomy, are lesser circles of the sphere, parallel to the ecliptic, and imagined to pass through every degree and minute of the colures.

Parallels of Altitude, or Almucantars, are circles parallel to the horizon, imagined to pass through every degree and minute of the meridian between the horizon and zenith, and having their poles in the zenith.

Parallels of Declination, in Astronomy, are the same with parallels of latitude in geography.
