PHILIPPOPEL, a city of Turkey in Europe, in the government of Rumelia, and in the department of Sofia, the Turkish name of which is Filibé. It stands upon a plain, watered by the river Maritza, which is to a certain extent navigable, in a beautiful situation, on the great road from the’frontiers to Constantinople. It is a well-built place, and has twenty mosques, two caravanserais, several Greek and Armenian churches, and many public and private baths. It is the seat of a Greek archbishop, and also a manufacturing city, in which woollen, silk, and cotton goods are made; and there are some very extensive tanneries. The commodities thus produced form a portion of the exports, to which are added raw materials, such as wool and silk.
