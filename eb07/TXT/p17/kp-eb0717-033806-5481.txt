PHALERAE, amongst the ancient Romans, were military rewards bestowed for signal acts of bravery. Authors are not agreed whether the phalerae were a suit of rich trappings for a horse, or golden chains something like the torques i but so formed as to hang down to the breast, and display a greater profusion of ornament.' The latter opinion appears to be the more prevalent one, but both are perhaps true.
