EXTRAVASATION, in contusions, fissures, depressions, fractures, and other accidents of the cranium, is when one or more of the blood-vessels, which are distributed in the dura mater, is broken or divided, by which means there is such a discharge of blood as greatly oppresses the brain, and disturbs its office, frequently bringing on violent pains, and at length death itself, unless the patient be timeously relieved.
