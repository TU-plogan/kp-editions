ERROR, in Philosophy, a mistake of the judgment, or giving assent to that which is not true. Mr Locke reduces the causes of error into these four: first, want of proofs; secondly, want of ability to use them; thirdly, want of will to use them; and, fourthly, wrong measures of probability.
