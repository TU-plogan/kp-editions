ENNIS, the chief town of the county of Clare, in Ireland. It is situated on the west bank of the river Fergus, and consists of two chief streets, one being parallel to the river, over which there are three bridges. There are no manufactures, and the only trade is in grain and cattle. It returns one member to parliament. The population of the borough amounted in 1831 to 9727.
