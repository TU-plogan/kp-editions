EPITROPUS, a kind of judge, or rather an arbitrator, which the Greek Christians, under the dominion of the Turks, were in use to elect in the several cities, in order to terminate the differences that arose among them, and thus avoid carrying them before the Turkish magistrates. See Arbitrator.
