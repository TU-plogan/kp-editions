EPOPOEIA, in Poetry, the history, action, or fable, which forms the subject of an epic poem. The word is derived from the Greek νχος, carmen, verse, and πro∕sw, facio, I make. In the common use of the word, however, epopoeia is the same with epos, or epic poem itself. See Poetry.
