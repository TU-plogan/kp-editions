GOUDA, a city of the Netherlands, in the province of Holland and circle of Rotterdam. It is situated on the river Gauwe, which flows out of the Yssel. It is well built, and has a considerable trade in making serges and baize; but the chief manufacture is that of pottery ware, and especially of tobacco pipes, which affords occupation to more than 5000 persons. It contains five churches: that of St [7:10:622] John is distinguished for one of the most powerful organs in Holland. The inhabitants are 12,000. Long. 4. 35.50. E. Lat. 51.50. 51. N.
