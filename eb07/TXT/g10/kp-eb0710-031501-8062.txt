GAP, an arrondissement of the department of the Upper Alps, in France, extending over 867 square miles. It is divided into fourteen cantons and 126 communes, and inhabited by 69,034 persons. The capital, a city of the same name,"is situated on the river Luie, on a kind of basin formed by hills, over which tower the snow-covered mountains of the Alps. It is an old and ill-built place, containing 7854 inhabitants, who are occupied in making linen and woollen cloths. There is in the city a museum, containing collections of the plants, minerals, birds, and antiquities of the Alps. Long. 5. 59. 42. E. Lat. 44. 33. 37. Åƒ.
