GENLIS, or Sends, an arrondissement of the department of the Oise, in France, extending over 467 square miles. It is divided into seven cantons, and these into 132 communes, inhabited by 78,790 persons. The capital, of the same name, is a city situated on the river Nonnette. It is an ill-built place. The cathedral has the loftiest tower of any in France. It contains nine other churches, two hospitals, with 940 dwellings, and 5016 inhabitants. It has manufactories of linen, lace, leather, cutlery, and some cotton spinning. Long. 2. 29. 53. E. Lat. 49. 12. 8. N.
