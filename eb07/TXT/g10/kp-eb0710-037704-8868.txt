GEISLINGEN, a bailiwick of the kingdom of Wurtemberg, in the circle of the Danube. It extends over 134 square miles, and comprehends three cities, three towns, and sixty villages, with 22,680 inhabitants. The capital, of the same name, on the Rolirbache, contains 400 houses and 2240 inhabitants, who are famed for their wood, horn, and ivory turnery ware, and part of whom are employed in cotton spinning by means of machinery, and also in making the machinery.
