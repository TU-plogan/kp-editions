CAROLUS, an ancient English broad piece of gold struck under Charles I. Its value at the time it was coined is said to have been twenty shillings.

Carolus, a small copper coin, with a little silver mixed in it, struck under Charles VIII. of France. The Carolus was worth twelve deniers when it ceased to be current.
