CHESHAM, a market-town in the hundred of Burnham, of the county of Buckingham, twenty-eight miles from London. It is situated in a pleasing vale. The chief employment, besides agriculture, is making bone lace. The market is held on Wednesday. The population amounted in 1811 to 1191, in 1821 to 2017, and in 1831 to 5388.
