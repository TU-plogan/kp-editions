CHRISM (from γjpω, I anoint), oil consecrated by the bishop, and used in the Roman Catholic and Greek churches, in the administration of baptism, confirmation, ordination, and extreme unction. It is prepared on Holy Thursday with much ceremony.

The chrism in baptism is performed by the priest, and in confirmation by the bishop. That used in ordination is more usually styled unction. Chrism Pence, Chrismatis Denarii, or Ciirismales Denarii, a tribute anciently paid to. the bishop by the parish clergy, for their chrism, consecrated at Easter for the ensuing year. This tribute was afterwards condemned as simoniacal.
