CIRCUMVALLATION, or Line of Circumvαllα-tion, in the art of war, is a trench bordered with a parapet, and thrown up quite round the besiegers’ camp. It is formed by way of security against any army that may attempt to relieve the place, and also serves to prevent desertion.
