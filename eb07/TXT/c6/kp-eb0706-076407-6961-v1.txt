CLITOMACHUS, a philosopher who flourished about 140 years before Christ. He was born at Carthage, but quitted his country at forty years of age, and went to Athens,where he became the disciple and successor of Carneades. He composed many books, but they were all lost CLITUMNUS, in Ancient Geography, a river of Umbria, on this side of the Apennines. According to Pliny, it was a fountain consisting of several veins, situated between[7:6:765] Hispellum and Spolctium, which soon after swelled into a navigable river, running from east to west into the Tinia, and both together into the Tiber. This river was famous for its milk-white flocks and herds. The god of the river was called Clitumnus.
