CASATI, Pauł, a learned Jesuit, born at Placentia in [7:6:211]1617. He entered early among the Jesuits; and after having taught mathematics and divinity at Rome, was sent into Sweden to Queen Christina, whom he prevailed on to embrace the popish religion. He wrote, 1. Vacuum proscriptum·, 2. Terra machinis mota, Rome, 1668, 4to; 3. Mechanicoum libri octo ; 4. De Igne Dissertationes, Parma, 1686 and 1695, 2 vols. 4to; 5. De Angelis Disputatio Theologica; 6. Hydrostaticae Dissertationes; 7. Opticae Disputationes. It is remarkable that he wrote this treatise on optics at the age of eighty-eight, and after he was blind. He also wrote several books in Italian.
