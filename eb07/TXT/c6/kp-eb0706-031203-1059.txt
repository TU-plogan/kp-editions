CHALUS, a city of the department of the Upper Vienne, in France. It is situated on the river Taboire, which divides it into two portions. It contains 1264 inhabitants, and is celebrated as the place where our Richard Coeur-de-Lion met his death by an arrow when engaged in besieging it. Long. 0. 53. 23. E. Lat. 45. 39. 31. N.
