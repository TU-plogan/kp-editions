CHITLONG, a small town of Northern Hindustan, consisting of a few brick and tiled houses, two or three stories high. It is said to have been formerly more flourishing; and is the first place that has the appearance of a town to the traveller coming from the south. The climate, owing to the height of the ground, is cold; and ice and snow are frequently seen. Long. 85. 52. E. Lat. 27. 29. N.
