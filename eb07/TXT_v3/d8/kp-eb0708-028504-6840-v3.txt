DUNWICH, a small borough-town of the hundred of Blything, in Suffolk, once a city, and remarkable only from having sent two members to parliament, but it is now disfranchised. The inhabitants amounted in 1801 to 184, in 1811 to 208, in 1821 to 200, and in 1831 to 232.
