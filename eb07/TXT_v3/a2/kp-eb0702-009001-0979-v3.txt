ACCOUNT, or Accompt, in a genera! sense, a computation or reckoning of any thing by numbers.—Collectively, it is used to express the books which merchants, traders, bankers, &c. use for recording their transactions in business.

Chamber of ACCOUNTS, in the French polity, a sovereign court of great antiquity, which took cognizance of and registered the accounts of the king’s revenue; nearly the same with the English Court of Exchequer.
