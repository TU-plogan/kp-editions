FRONTISPIECE, in Architecture, the principal face of a fine building. The word is formed of the Latin frontis-picium, q. d. frontis hominis inspectio. Hence also, by a figure, we say, the frontispiece of a book, meaning an ornament with an engraven title on the first page.
