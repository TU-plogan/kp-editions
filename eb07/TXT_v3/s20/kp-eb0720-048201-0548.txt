SORRENTO, a city of Italy, in the province of Naples. It is finely situated among olive, citron, and pomegranate trees, and is the seat of an archbishop. It is on the sea coast, and has some foreign trade. The chief occupation is collecting and spinning silk. It was the birthplace of the poet Tasso. The surrounding hills are chiefly compos ed of tufa, which was originally lava; and the doors and windowseats are generally formed of that substance. It contains 4240 inhabitants.
