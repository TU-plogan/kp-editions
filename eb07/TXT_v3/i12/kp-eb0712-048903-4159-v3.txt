ITHACA, in Ancient Geography, an island in the Ionian Sea, on the coast of Epirus, the country of Ulysses, with a town and port situated at the foot of Mount Neius. According to Pliny, it is about twenty-five miles in compass, and according to Artemidorus only ten; but it is now found to be seventeen miles long and four broad. See article Ionian Isles.
