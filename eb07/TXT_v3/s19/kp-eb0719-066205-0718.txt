SAVU, an island in the Eastern Seas, about twenty miles in length, low at the sea-coast, but towards the middle rising into considerable hills. According to Captain Cook and other navigators, it presents a delightful prospect from the sea. The inhabitants have made some approaches to civilization, and the island is divided into five principalities. Long. 122. 30. E. Lat 10. 35. S.
