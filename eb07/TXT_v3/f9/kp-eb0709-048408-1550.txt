FAME, a heathen goddess, celebrated chiefly by the poets. She is feigned to have been the last of the race o Titians produced by the earth, to have had her palace m [7:9:485]the air, and to have possessed a vast number of eyes, ears, and tongues. She is mentioned by Hesiod, and particularly described by both Virgil and Ovid.
