FLOOD, a deluge or inundation of waters. See the article Deluge. Flood is also used in speaking of the tide. When the water is at the lowest, it is called ebb ; when rising, young flood ; when at the highest, high flood; when beginning to fall, ebb water. ELooD-marh, the mark which the sea makes on the shore at flowing water and the highest tide. It is also called high-water mark.
