MAYORGA, a cluster of three or four small islands, the largest about sixty miles in circuit, in the South Pacific Ocean. They were discovered in 1780 by a Spanish frigate. Long. 179∙ 52. E. Lat. 18. 38. S.

ΜΑΖΑ, amongst the Athenians, was a sort of cake made of flour boiled with water and oil, and set as the common fare before those who were entertained at the public expense in the common hall or Prytaneum.
