LANGUEDOC was, previously to the revolution, the nameof a large province of France, twohundredand twenty miles in length, by one hundred and twenty in breadth. It was divided into Upper and Lower Languedoc, Toulouse being the capital of the former, and Montpellier, so celebrated for the salubrity of its climate, the capital of the latter.
