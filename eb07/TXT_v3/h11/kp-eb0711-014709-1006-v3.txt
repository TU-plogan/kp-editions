HARLESTON, a town of Norfolk, in the hundred of Earsham, a portion of the parish of Redenhall. It is situated on the river Waveney, 100 miles from London, and there is a good corn market on Thursday. The population amounted in 1801 to 1459, in 1811 to 1538, in 1821 to 1641, and in 1831 to 1784.
