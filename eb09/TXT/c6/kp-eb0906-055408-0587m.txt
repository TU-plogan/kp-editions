CRAUFORD, Quentin (1743-1819), an English author. In early life he went to India, where he entered the British army, and on the conclusion of peace devoted himself to commerce. Returning to Europe before the age of forty with a handsome fortune, he settled at Paris, where he gave himself to the cultivation of literature and art, and formed a good library and collection of paintings, coins, and other objects of antiquarian interest, and where he remained till his death, with the exception of ten years from the outbreak of the Revolution to the Peace of Amiens.
