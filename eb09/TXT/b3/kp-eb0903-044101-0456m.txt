BATHURST, a town of New South Wales, on the Macquarie River, 122 miles W.S.W. of Sydney, with which it is connected by railway. It stands in a fertile plain on the western side of the Blue Mountains, and is the centre of an important gold field. Founded in 1815 by Governor Macquarie, and named in honour of Lord Bathurst (the third earl), it soon became a place of considerable size, and was raised to the rank of a municipality in 1862. It is built in rather a spacious style, with broad and regular streets running at right angles, Many of the buildings are large and handsome; and it possesses numerous churches and schools, a theatre, a hospital, and various societies. Population in 1871, 5030.
