BARR, a town in Alsace, 18 miles S.W. of Strasburg, situated on the eastern slope of the Vosges, at the mouth of the Ulrichthal. Wool and cotton spinning, and the manufacture of pottery, crystal, and soap, are its principal industries; and an active trade is carried on in wine, brandy, vinegar, cattle, and wood. The town is mentioned as early as the 8th century. It was burned by the troops of the Cardinal of Lorraine in 1592; in 1678 it suffered from a severe conflagration; andin 1794 it was greatly damaged by the explosion of the arsenal. There is a tepid mineral spring in the neighbourhood, and, on the ()dilienberg, which rises above the town, are the ruins of the convent of St Odilia, which was founded in the 7th century Population, 5651.
