BILSA, a town of Hindustan, in the territory of Gwâlior or the possessions of Sindhiâ, situated on the Betwâ River in lat. 23° 30' N. and long. 77° 50' E. It is enclosed with a stone wall, and defended by square towers and a ditch. The suburbs without the walls are not very extensive, but the streets are spacious, and contain some good houses. The town and the surrounding country are celebrated all over India for the excellent quality of the tobacco, which is bought up with great eagerness and exported. Population about 3000. Distance south from Gwâlior, 190 miles.
