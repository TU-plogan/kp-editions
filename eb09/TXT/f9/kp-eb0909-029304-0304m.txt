FLAVIGNY, Valerien, was born near Laon about the beginning of the 17 th century. Having studied in the college of Sorbonne, he received from it his doctor’s diploma in 1628, and shortly afterwards obtained a canonry in the cathedral of Rheims. In 1630 he became professor of Hebrew in the college of France. He died at Paris, 29th April 1674. The works of Flavigny are chiefly occupied with discussions regarding the Hebrew text of the Bible. His opinions acquired him for a time considerable celebrity, and engaged him in controversies with some of the doctors of the Sorbonne, But now possess scarcely any interest.
