FLOYER, Sir John (1649-1734), M.D., physician and author, was born at Hinters in Staffordshire, and was educated at Oxford. He practised in Lichfield, and it was by his advice that Dr Johnson, when a child, was taken by his mother to be touched by Queen Anne for the king’s evil, March 30, 1714. Dr Johnson had a high opinion of his learning and piety. Floyer died February 1, 1734. Besides letters and other short pieces in various publications, he wrote—
