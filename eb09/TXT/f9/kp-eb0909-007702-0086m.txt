FERDINAND. The name of Ferdinand (the Italian Ferdinαηdo or Ferrante, Spanish Fernando or Hernando, Arabian Ferdeland), which is supposed to be of Gothic origin and to be allied to the German verdienend (meritorious), has been borne by a considerable number of European sovereigns, the more important of whom are noticed below in the following order—first, the emperors, and then the kings of Naples, Portugal, and Spain, the grand-dukes of Tuscany, and the duke of Brunswick.
