BUDDAeUS, John Francis (1667-1729), a celebrated Lutheran divine, and one of the most learned men Germany has produced, was born at Anklam, a town of Pomerania, where his father was minister. He studied with great distinction at Greifswald and at Wittenberg, and having attained to eminence in languages, theology, and history, was appointed Greek and Latin professor at Coburg, afterwards professor of ethical science and politics in the university of Halle, and at length, in 1705, professor of divinity at Jena, where, after having acquired a very great reputation, he died in 1729.
