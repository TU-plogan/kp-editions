ALCIRA, probably the Soetabicula of the Romans, a Spanish town, on an island in the river Xucar, 25 miles S.W. of Valencia, in the province of that name. It is surrounded by walls, and has two fine bridges. There is a remarkable stalactite grotto in the vicinity. The principal productions are silk, rice, and oranges, which are largely exported. Population, 15,400.
