ALDAN, a river of Siberia, in the government of Yakutsk, which rises about 55° N. lat., and 125° E. long., and after flowing more than 300 miles in a north-east direction, turns to the north-west, joining the Lena about 100 miles below Yakutsk. It has a total length of over 500 miles, for a considerable part of which it is navigable.
