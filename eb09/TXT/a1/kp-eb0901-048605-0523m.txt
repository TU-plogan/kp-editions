ALEXANDER JANNAeUS, king of the Jews, succeeded his brother Aristobulus in 104 b.c., and died in 79 B.c. His reign, which he commenced by putting to death one of his brothers who claimed the throne, was disgraced by the cruelties that he perpetrated in order to keep himself in power.
