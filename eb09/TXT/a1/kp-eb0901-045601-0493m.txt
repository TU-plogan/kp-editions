ALBUERA, a small village of Spain, in the province of Badajoz, 13 miles S.E. of the town of that name. It is celebrated on account of the victory gained there on the 16th of May 1811 by the English, Portuguese, and Spaniards, under Marshal Beresford, over the French army commanded by Marshal Soult.
