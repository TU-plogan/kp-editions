ALVA, a village in Stirlingshire, Scotland, situated at the foot of Craigleith, one of the Ochil range, 7 miles N.E. of Stirling, with which it is connected by railway. Besides the parish church, there are places of worship belonging to the Free and United Presbyterian churches. Yam spinning and the manufacture of shawls and tweeds are carried on to a considerable extent. Population in 1871, 4096.
