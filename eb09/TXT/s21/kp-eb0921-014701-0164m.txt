SAGAR, principal town and headquarters of the above district, situated in 23° 50' N. lat. and 78° 49' E. long., is well built with wide streets and stands on the borders of a small but beautiful lake, and has military cantonments. Ságar is the entrepôt of the salt trade with Rajputána, and carries on a large trade with Mírzápur district in the North-Western Provinces, importing sugar and other grocery, besides English cloth. The population of the town in 1881 was 44,416 (males 22,556, females 21,860).
