KUTY, a municipal town in the Austrian province of Galicia, lies 20 miles south-east of Kolomea, and on the left bank of the Czeremosz, which here forms the boundary between Galicia and Bukowina, in 48° 16' N. lat., 25° 10' E. long. The trade, especially in prepared leather, is chiefly with Hungary and the northern or Moldavian portion of Roumania. The neighbourhood of Kuty is picturesque and mountainous, and has productive salt springs. Population 8579, mostly of Armenian, Ruthenian, Polish, and J ewish extraction. Kuty formerly belonged to the old province of Ruthenia, in the kingdom of Poland.
