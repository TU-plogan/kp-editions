GUALEGUAY, a town of the Argentine republic, department of Entre-Rios, is situated on the Gualeguay river, which falls into the Parana, and is navigable up to this point, 120 miles N.N.W. of Buenos Ayres. It is a prosperous and increasing town, and has an active shipping trade in beef, mutton, hides, wool, tallow, and timber. In 1874 the total value of exports was Â£268,970.
