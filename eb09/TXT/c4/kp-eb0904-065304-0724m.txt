CALCAR, or Kalcker, John de (1499-1546), an eminent painter, born at Calcar, in the duchy of Cleves, in 1499. He was a disciple of Titian at Venice, and perfected himself by studying Raffaelle. He imitated those masters with such success as to deceive the most skilful critics. Among his various pieces is a Nativity, representing the angels around the infant Christ, which he arranged so that the light emanated wholly from the child. lie died at Naples in 1546.
