CANCALE, a seaport town of France, in the department of Ille-et-Vilaine, 10 miles E. of St Malo, on the bay of St Michael. A considerable trade is carried on in oysters, which are found in the bay in great numbers and of excellent quality. In 1758 the duke of Marlborough disembarked an army of 14,000 English here for the purpose of attacking St Malo, but retired without accomplishing anything. Population in 1872, 3814.
