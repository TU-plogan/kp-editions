LEIAH, a town in Derâ Ismâil Khan district, Punjab, India, is situated near the east bank of the Indus, in 30° 57' 30" N. lat., 70° 58' 20'' E. long. The population in 1868 was 17,033 (13,151 Mohammedans, 3726 Hindus, 30 Sikhs, and 126 “others”). There is a considerable trade in local produce as well as through traffic between the districts of upper India and the countries to the west.
