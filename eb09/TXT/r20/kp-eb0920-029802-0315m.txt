RÁWAL PINDÍ or Rawul Pindee, principal town and administrative headquarters of the above district, lies in 33° 37' N. lat. and 73° 6' E. long. The .present town is of modern origin; it is well built and has an air of considerable prosperity; its streets are broad and handsome, and several fine buildings add to its appearance. It is chiefly a grain mart. The population of the town in 1881 was 52,975 (35,985 males and 16,990 females).
