CARLSTADT, a royal free city of Austrian Croatia, in the comitat of Agram, the capital of the district of the same name, stands on the Ku]pa, which here receives the Korana and the Dobra, 32 miles south-west of Agram. It consists of the fortress (which is surrounded by ramparts, trenches, and palisades), the inner town, and a suburb. Carlstadt is the seat of a Greek bishop, and five Catholic churches, a Catholic gymnasium, and many handsome public buildings. It has a considerable transit trade, and manufactures the liqueur called rosoglio. It is situated in 45° 27' N. lat. and 15° 36' E. long. Population (1871), 5515.
