CHIAVARĪ, a maritime town of North Italy, in the province of Genoa, and 21 miles E.S.E. of the town of that name, on the Gulf of Rapallo, at the mouth of the Sturla. It has a hospital, a railway station, and several fine churches, the principal of which is that of the Madonna del’ Orto. The Genoa and Sarzano railway passes the town. The inhabitants, numbering about 11,500, are engaged in the manufacture of furniture, silk twist, lace, and linen, and in the anchovy fishery. The surrounding hills are well cultivated, and slate and marble are quarried in the neighbourhood.
