CAUDETE, a town of Spain, in the province of Albacete, about 80 miles north of Cartagena. It was formerly fortified, and contains a town-house, a prison, a school of primary instruction, a Carmelite convent, and several other religious institutions. The inhabitants are engaged almost exclusively in agriculture and the manufacture of coarse fabrics for home consumption. Population, 6500.
