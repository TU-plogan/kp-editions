PHILO of BYZANTIUM, author of a treatise on mechanics, of which only two books now remain, flourished in the 2d or 3d century a.d. The extant books, which refer to machines used in war and to siege works, are edited with a German translation in Köchly and Rüstow’s Griechische Kriegsschriftsteller, vol. i. (Leipsic, 1853).

For a list of other Philos, see Fabricius, Bibi. Graeca, iv. p. 750 sq., ed. Hari.
